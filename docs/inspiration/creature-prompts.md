## Early Campaign Creatures

- A territorial beast that has grown unusually aggressive after its hunting grounds were disturbed.
- A pack predator that uses numbers and coordination rather than raw strength.
- A scavenger drawn to battlefields and mass graves.
- A burrowing creature undermining roads and foundations near a settlement.
- A once-harmless animal warped by prolonged exposure to a corrupted site.
- An undead animal animated imperfectly, driven more by instinct than malice.

## Established Campaign Creatures

- A creature that has learned to ambush armed travelers instead of easy prey.
- A guardian beast trained to recognize uniforms, symbols, or scents.
- A monster that claims a ruin or chokepoint and defends it relentlessly.
- A pack leader whose presence changes how lesser creatures behave.
- A stalking predator that withdraws once injured, returning when its prey is weakest.
- An undead brute used as a living siege engine or shock troop.

## Late Campaign Creatures

- An ancient predator whose territory has gone unchallenged for generations.
- A colossal creature whose movement alone reshapes the battlefield.
- A construct or engineered monster designed for a single overwhelming purpose.
- A horror that adapts mid-fight after observing its enemies.
- A legendary beast tied to a place, curse, or long-forgotten crime.
- An apex monster whose defeat would permanently change the region.

## Story-Driven Wildcards

- A creature that appears singular but is only the first of its kind encountered.
- A monster intended to flee, survive, and become a recurring threat later.
